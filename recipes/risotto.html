Risotto

Description

A creamy, comforting bowl of mushroom risotto is the perfect dish on a chilly winter evening. With just a little rice, some mushrooms, a bit of alium, veggie stock, and cheese, you'll have a dish sure to thaw even the most frozen belly. And, using whatever veggies, proteins, and herbs you have on hand, you can create an almost endless variety. Follow the recipe below to learn how to make a filling risotto sure to be a hit with the whole family.

Ingredients

  • 4 Cups Vegetable Stock
  • 3 Tbsp Olive Oil
  • 2 Tbsp Butter
  • 1 Cup Arborio Rice
  • 8 oz Mushroom, chopped
  • 2 Shallots, diced
  • 3 Cloves of Garlic, minced
  • 1 Cup White Wine
  • 1/2 Cup Parmesean Cheese
  • 1 Tbsp Fresh Rosemary, minced
  • Salt and Pepper to taste

Steps

  1. In a small sauce pan, bring Vegetable Stock to a boil, and then reduce temperature to low.
  2. Heat a large sautee pan over medium-high heat. Once warm, add 1 Tbsp Olive Oil.
  3. Add Mushrooms to pan and sautee. Cook mushrooms until brown and mushrooms have purged their liquid. Put mushrooms and liquid in a bowl and set aside.
  4. Add 2 Tbsp Butter and remaining 2 Tbsp Olive Oil to pan. Add Shallots and sautee for 2 minutes.
  5. Add Garlic and sautee until shallots begin to turn transluscent
  6. Add Arborio Rice and Rosemary to the pan and sautee, stirring occasionally, until rice is appears toasted and begins to brown.
  7. Carefully add white Wine to the pan and deglaze. Cook until all wine has absorbed into rice.
  8. Add 1 Ladle of Vegetable Stock to the rice, stirring and deglazing, before allowing to simmer. Simmer until liquid has been absorbed. Then repeat this step until less than 1 ladle of stock remains.
  9. Add Parmesean Cheese, mushrooms and their reserved liquid, and remaining vegetable stock to the pan and stir well.
  10. Add salt and pepper to taste.
  11. Serve warm and enjoy!
